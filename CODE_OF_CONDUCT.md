# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We are committed to creating a welcoming, inclusive, and harassment-free environment for everyone participating in this project, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual orientation.

We aim to foster a positive and respectful community where all contributors and users feel safe to participate, share ideas, and collaborate.

---

## Expected Behavior

All participants in this project are expected to:

- Be respectful, considerate, and constructive in discussions and feedback  
- Use welcoming and inclusive language  
- Respect differing viewpoints and experiences  
- Accept constructive criticism gracefully  
- Focus on what is best for the project and the learning goals of the team  
- Show empathy towards other community members  

---

## Unacceptable Behavior

Unacceptable behavior includes, but is not limited to:

- Harassment, discrimination, or hateful conduct of any kind  
- Offensive, abusive, or derogatory comments  
- Personal attacks, insults, or trolling  
- Publishing others’ private information without explicit permission  
- Any behavior that creates a hostile or unsafe environment  

---

## Reporting and Enforcement

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it to the project maintainers.

**Contact:**  
You may report incidents by opening a private communication with the maintainers via GitHub (e.g., private message or email, if provided in the repository), or by contacting the course instructional team if the issue involves team members.

All reports will be reviewed in good faith and handled as confidentially as possible. The maintainers may take appropriate action, including warnings, temporary restrictions, or removal from participation in the project, depending on the severity of the behavior.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces, including GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, code reviews, documentation, and any related communication channels associated with this project.
